Pomodoro Technique 番茄鐘工作法

Pomodoro techinique, which is also known as tomato timer technique, is a well-known time managing method cited by Francesco Cirillo.
The core idea of Pomodoro techinique is to tear time into small pieces. The most common way is to divide usable time into 25-min unit, and people are more likely to focus on things on hands in this short period of time.

Tomato Timer

However, the actual application of pomodoro techinique is not quite easy. There are some points that people should pay extreme attention to when trying pomodoro techinique.
First, before dividing the time, the first step is to calculate today's usable hours. For example, even though I work 8 hours a day, there could be one hour of meeting and another hour of job-assigning, and not to mention that I sometimes spend a little time chatting with colleague. After deducting the taken time, there comes the real usable time.
Second, instead of putting everything you have to do into the schedule, try to list things out and specify the deadlines. The next step is to estimate how many 25-min units you need to finish each tasks, and them arrange time into today's scedule with the consideration fo their emergency levels.
Here we get the elementary pomodoro scedule. With this small-unit secdule, we can definitely work with higher productivity. I believe distrctions would be willing to wait for just a few minutes.
